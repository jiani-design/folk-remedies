    Cold

    Explore those cultural home remedies to see how cold can be treated differently.

    ×

    Mexico: Cinnamon Tea/Té de Canela

    Cinnamon tea, also called Té de Canela in Mexico, is a conventional homemade drink remedy that relieves cold and sore throat symptoms.

    Ingredients:

    • Canela sticks
    • Water
    • Honey

    Steps:

    1. Steep the cinnamon in water.
    2. Boil the water with cinnamon for 3 to 5 minutes.
    3. Leave the hot water for 30 minutes
    4. Sweeten the cinnamon with honey (not necessary)

    Africa: Lemon Leaves Steam

    Lemon Leaves Steam is a practice in African culture that helps ease breathing and clear nasal  passage. Lemon leaves are applied as ingredients of this hot steam.

    Ingredients:

    • Fresh lemon leaves
    • Water

    Steps:

    1. Clean the lemon leaves and cut the lemons into chunks.
    2. Boil the water with lemon leaves and lemons.
    3. Transfer the leaves to a basin, and mix the boiled water with the leaves.
    4. Cover yourself with a blanket, and use the basin as a steamer.

    Eastern Europe: Mouse Rinse

    Mouth rinse with salt and soda is a quick remedy in Eastern European households that helps ease a sore throat immediately.

    Ingredients:

    • Hot Water
    • Salt
    • Soda

    Steps:

    1. Prepare a glass of hot water
    2. Add 1 table spoon of salt to the water.
    3. Add another 2 tablespoons of soda to the water.
    4. Stir the water and gargle it, and don't drink it; you will feel better soon with your throat in a quick second.
